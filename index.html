Our Philosophy

“We learn French we catch English”

The combination of Montessori and Reggio philosophy gives us the opportunity to plan a curriculum that emphasizes children’s multiple intelligences. We believe that learning “languages” that combines arts and academics will allow children to develop an understanding of the world and appreciate the discovery of cultures & traditions. Expert teachers will guide each child through activities building upon children’s Cognitive, Social, Emotional skills and Creativity. We consider each child as a whole and as capable when planning our curriculum. Investigation, Exploration, Physical, and Social development of the child are our major focus. Children will go into the world with the necessary knowledge to problem solve and become creative thinkers and accomplished individuals.

Our Curriculum and Programs

At Noisy Monkeys Academy program environemnt, these six distinct areas constitute the prepared environment:

Languages arts

Includes oral language development, written expression, reading, the study of grammar, creative dramatics and children’s literature...

Mathematics

After setting up the environment, we make use of manipulative Montessori materials such as: Coloured beads, Golden Bar, Hundred board, Number rods etc… these materials will enable the child to internalize concepts.

Fine Arts

Children naturally love music, painting, dancing and other creative ways to express themselves.We believe, by giving the children opportunities to be creative through fine art activities they will develop motor skills...

Practical life

The purpose of practical life exercise is to help the child gain control in the coordination of his movement, gain independence or adapt to his society. The child learn such as pouring, folding, carrying, dusting a table or sweeping...

Social Sciences

Expose the child to basics of Geography, History and Life Sciences. Exploring topics related to his environment, his community and the world around him. The child will build cultural awareness, by discovering traditions and diversity in a global world.

Physical Activity

Physical activity is recommended for the healthy development of the child. Movement is the more important concept attached to the child’s learning and his well-being. When children move throughout the day they are more likely to self-regulate in the environment they dwelt in.
